\section{Introduction}
The demand for effective high-voltage protective barrier coatings is driven by the need to enhance safety and reliability near power lines. Inorganic materials with specific stability, electronic, and physical properties are of interest for such applications. This study focuses on identifying materials that meet these criteria through a systematic screening process.

\section{Discussion}
The retrieved set shows a range of stable and near-stable materials with wide band gaps and suitable densities for high-voltage applications. The plots provide insights into the relationships between stability, electronic, and physical properties. However, additional validation would be required to confirm the suitability of these materials for practical applications, as this study does not include thermal transport, melting point, oxidation kinetics, or mechanical validation.

\section{Conclusion}
This study successfully retrieved and characterized a set of 100 inorganic materials based on specified stability, electronic, and physical property criteria. The results provide a foundation for further exploration and validation of these materials for high-voltage protective barrier coatings.
